# The game

## Game contents

1. A deck of cards with various illustrations.
   1. A golden coin
   2. A diamond
   3. The monkey & parrot
   4. Peace
   5. The skull
      1. Comes in 2 variants, either with a 1 or 2 skulls.
   6. Shipbreak
   7. The treasure chest
   8. Zombie outbreak
   9. The pirate
   10. The guard
   11. The battleship
       1. Comes in variants of 2, 3 or 4 sables.
2. A set of 8 die with 6 faces.
   1. A coin
   2. A diamond
   3. A parrot
   4. A monkey
   5. A skull
   6. A sable

## Game preparation

Shuffle the deck of cards and place them face down on the table.

## Rules

### Setup

Each player receives an overview card showing the possible dice combinations and their point values. Shuffle the remaining GAME cards and place them face-down as a deck on the table. The game plays clockwise.

### The Dangers of Dice

After the initial roll of all 8 dice and depending on the card drawn, the player decides which dice to set aside and which to re-roll. The player is not required to set aside any dice; they may re-roll any die, even ones they previously set aside.

For re-rolling, two restrictions apply:

- A re-roll must be done with at least 2 dice. Rolling with only 1 die is not permitted, unless a card explicitly allows you to.
- A die showing a skull may not be re-rolled. It remains "forbidden" for the rest of that player's turn.

**Important:** If a player rolls a third skull, their turn ends immediately. They score 0 points, regardless of their other dice results.

### Scoring at the End of a Turn

To earn points, the player must voluntarily end their turn before rolling a third skull. They then count the values of their combinations and individual dice.

**Combinations:**

- 3 of a kind: 100 points
- 4 of a kind: 200 points
- 5 of a kind: 500 points
- 6 of a kind: 1,000 points
- 7 of a kind: 2,000 points
- 8 of a kind: 4,000 points

**Diamonds and Gold Coins:** These provide double points for both combinations and for individual symbols (100 points each).

**Full Treasure Chest Bonus:** If a player receives points for all 8 dice, they receive a bonus of 500 additional points. (This bonus is not possible with a skull die.)

### Skull Island

If a player rolls 4 or more skulls in their initial roll of all 8 dice, they "sail to Skull Island." They set aside the skulls and re-roll the remaining dice. As long as the player rolls at least 1 skull and sets it aside, they may continue rolling.

In this case, other symbols have no function. If no skull is rolled, the turn ends. The player scores 0 points. However, every other player collects 100 penalty points per skull rolled. A player's score can go below 0.

### Pirate Cards

Pirate cards modify players' turns and provide bonus or penalty points:

- **Treasure Island:** After a roll, the player may place 1 or more dice on this card. They may take these dice back later and re-roll them. If the player rolls a third skull, their turn ends and they cannot place any dice from that roll on the card. They only receive points for dice on the card.

- **Skull:** The player's turn begins with 1 or 2 skulls already rolled (depending on the card). These count as rolled skulls. The player cannot earn a bonus and has a higher chance of sailing to Skull Island.

- **Pirate:** The player doubles all points scored in this turn. If they sail to Skull Island, other players each lose 200 points per skull rolled.

- **Guard:** The player may re-roll one rolled skull during their turn. In this case, a skull die can be rolled individually.

- **Pirate Ship:** The player must roll at least the shown number of sables and voluntarily end their turn. They receive the usual points (including for the sable combination) plus bonus points shown on the card. If they fail to roll the required number of sables, they score 0 points and must deduct the penalty points shown on the card. A player who draws "Pirate Ship" cannot sail to Skull Island.

- **Gold Coin:** The player's turn begins with a gold coin that counts both as an individual symbol and can be used in combinations.

- **Diamond:** The player's turn begins with a diamond that counts both as an individual symbol and can be used in combinations.

- **Animals:** Rolled monkeys and parrots count together as 1 combination. For example, 2 parrots and 3 monkeys would count as a combination of 5 identical dice.

## Win condition

The first player to reach 6,000 points wins the game. When a player reaches 6,000 or more points, every other player gets one more turn. If one of them exceeds the leading player's score, the player who initially reached 6,000 also gets one more turn.

**Special Rule - Pirate Magic:** A player who rolls a combination of 9 identical dice (for example, with a "Gold Coin" card and 8 gold coin dice) wins immediately.

**Note:** If no player has 6,000 or more points after the final turns, the game continues until a player reaches 6,000 points—they win immediately.

**Variant:** Players can agree at the start of a different point total for a shorter (e.g., 5,000 points) or longer (e.g., 8,000 points) game.

## Gameplay

On a player's turn:

1. The player flips the top pirate card from the deck and places it face-up on the pile.
2. The player rolls all 8 dice.
3. The drawn pirate card affects the player's rolls (see "Pirate Cards" in the Rules section).
4. On the first roll, all 8 dice must be rolled. After that, the player decides which dice to set aside and which to re-roll.
5. The player tries to roll as many matching symbols as possible.
6. Diamonds and gold coins are particularly valuable because they provide bonus points.
7. When the player ends their turn, they place the pirate card face-down on the discard pile and pass the dice to the next player.
8. The player's score is recorded on the scoresheet.

**Deck Management:** If the face-down deck is empty, shuffle the discard pile and use it as the new face-down deck.

## Example Turn

**First Roll:** The player rolls and gets: 1 monkey, 1 parrot, 3 sables, 2 gold coins, 1 skull, 1 diamond

The skull must be set aside. The player also decides to set aside the 3 sables and 2 gold coins. They re-roll the monkey and parrot.

**Second Roll:** They get 2 gold coins and 2 parrots.

Excellent! To score more points, they set aside the 2 additional gold coins and re-roll the 3 sables.

**Third Roll:** They get 1 skull and 2 more parrots.

Disappointing. Now they have 2 skulls rolled, so they don't take further risks and end their turn.

**Final Score:**

- 4 gold coins: 400 points
- 1 diamond: 100 points
- 1 combination of 4 identical symbols (monkeys + parrots): 200 points
- **Subtotal:** 700 points
- **Pirate card doubling:** ×2 = **1,400 points total**
